Class Experiences

at The University of Texas at Austin

Computer Science Curriculum

Class Sentiments

Most Rewarding

Computer Architecture is the most rewarding class I have taken at UT. I learned a lot and put forth a lot of effort, but I feel I learned more in CS 352 than in any other class.

Most Challenging

It's difficult to decide the most challenging course because it can easily be confused with the hardest course. Sometimes a course is only hard because it's not taught right, even though the material itself is not challenging. I would have to say CS 372: Operating Systems was my most challenging course, considering all those long nights spent in the computer lab before impending project deadlines.

Least Challenging

Once again, not to be confused with "easiest". My least challenging course at UT was CS 337: Theory in Programming Practice. A lot of the material had already been covered in prerequisite courses, and I was able to learn the new concepts effortlessly by studying the lecture slides for a short time. On top of that, the programming projects were fairly trivial.

Favorite

My favorite computer science course at UT has been CS 341: Automata Theory. Also one of the most difficult courses I've taken, the theoretical concepts interest me greatly, and I feel it will be one of my most valuable courses in the long term.

Least Favorite

By far, my least favorite computer science course at UT has been CS 347: Data Management. Not only was the material presented very poorly, the work was dull and tedious. While the homework and projects weren't hard, it took a great deal of motivation to get them completed.

Top Five

Superlatives out of the way, my top 5 courses in the Computer Science curriculum at UT have been the following.

  1. CS 341: Automata Theory
  2. CS 352: Computer Systems Architecture
  3. CS 307: Foundations of Computer Science
  4. CS 349: Contemporary Issues in Computer Science - W
  5. CS 336: Analysis of Programs

Friends
