Current issue

Vol 139 / Issue 18

  • JUST PUBLISHED in Development

    • Retinoic acid's developmental role illuminated

      Retinoic acid (RA) regulates numerous developmental processes. Bensimon and colleagues use photo-isomerisation to convert 13-cis RA to all-trans RA and vice versa to quantitatively and spatiotemporally manipulate RA activity in zebrafish embryos.

    • COP1 sheds light on root development

      Vernoux, Xu and colleagues uncover a novel long-distance mechanism by which light signals through the master photomorphogenesis repressor COP1 to modulate auxin transport coordinate root and shoot development in Arabidopsis.

    • Pancreatic development keeps its Sox on

      Sander and co-workers propose that a Sox9/Fgf feed-forward loop controls organ fate commitment and progenitor cell expansion in the developing pancreas. This loop may regulate cell identity and organ size in a broad spectrum of developmental and regenerative contexts.
