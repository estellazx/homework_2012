AVC’s 2012 Animal Welfare in Practice focuses on poultry welfare

Poultry welfare is the focus of AVC’s 2012 Animal Welfare in Practice conference, scheduled for September 14 to 15. Renowned animal welfare expert Dr. Ian Duncan will give a public talk on September 14 about welfare issues in commercial chicken production. Hosted by the Sir James Dunn Animal Welfare Centre and the AVC Animal Welfare Club, the conference is of interest to veterinary and animal science students, producers, veterinarians, animal scientists, and consumers. The registration deadline is September 7. Click here for more details. (Photo courtesy of Jane Morrigan)

 

Innovation PEI funds bitter crab disease and green crab research projects

Innovation PEI  recently awarded funding under its Pilot and Discovery Fund to researchers at AVC and UPEI for projects involving two species of crab. One project aims to identify the toxin responsible for bitter crab disease, a fatal disease of snow crabs (pictured above) and other crustaceans caused by a parasitic dinoflagellate. The second project will explore the potential of using the invasive European green crab as a feed ingredient for the aquaculture industry.
